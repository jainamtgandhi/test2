Translating Complex Product Features into Clear, Appealing, Buyer-Centric Messaging

This page shows how I use customer personas and their pain points to create clear, outcomes-driven messaging for an B2B SaaS company offering AI-based solution in supply chain space.

The Challenge

Current State
(Generic Framing)

Many B2B tech websites sound like investor decks - full of vision and architecture, but light on buyer outcomes.

The Gap

Executives and operators need to see clarity, value, and proof.

My Goal

Bridge this gap by applying a structured PMM framework.

ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

1. Firmographics

  • Industries: Consumer Packaged Goods (CPG), Retail, Food & Beverage, Pharma, Logistics/3PL.
  • Company Size: Fortune 500s + Large Enterprises (>$1B revenue, global operations).
  • Regions: Global, but especially North America, Europe, APAC multinationals.
  • Supply Chain Complexity: Multi-node networks (warehouses, plants, global suppliers).

2. Technographics

  • Likely using SAP, Oracle, Kinaxis, Blue Yonder, o9.
  • Already have data warehouses / cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure).
  • Some in-house data science, but struggling to scale.

3. Pain Points

  • Rigid legacy tools → hard to model new scenarios.
  • Forecasting still spreadsheet-heavy or black-box vendor locked.
  • Disruptions (tariffs, shipping delays, labor shortages) not planned fast enough.
  • Too slow to adapt to market shocks.

4. Buying Triggers

  • Recent disruption (COVID-style event, tariffs, strikes).
  • Cost pressures (margin squeeze, inflation).
  • New CIO/CSCO mandate → "modernize with AI."
  • Digital transformation initiatives.
  • Competitors already adopting advanced AI planning.

5. Value Drivers

  • Faster scenario modeling (hours → minutes).
  • More accurate forecasts → fewer stockouts/overstocks.
  • Better ROI on network planning decisions.
  • Empower teams to run models without waiting on IT.
  • Flexibility → not locked into rigid vendor logic.
